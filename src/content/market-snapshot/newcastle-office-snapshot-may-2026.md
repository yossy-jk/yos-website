# NEWCASTLE OFFICE MARKET SNAPSHOT
### May 2026 | Prepared by Your Office Space

---

## THE HEADLINE

Vacancy is falling and nothing new is coming. If your lease is up in the next 18 months, the window to negotiate hard is closing fast.

---

## VACANCY

Overall vacancy across Newcastle's office market sits at approximately **14.9%** — down from 16.4% a year ago. That's nearly two full percentage points of improvement in 12 months.

*Source: Property Council of Australia Office Market Report, early 2025. The Feb 2026 update confirms the downward trend is continuing with no new supply entering the market in 2026.*

The headline number doesn't tell the full story. A-grade buildings are near full. D-grade stock is the drag — vacancy there sat above 9% at last count and climbing, as tenants upgrade to better space and leave the older buildings behind.

City fringe (Hunter St, Parry St corridor) is tighter than the headline suggests. If you're looking for quality space in a good location, the real vacancy rate for what you'd actually want is well below 10%.

**What it means for tenants:** Less choice. Landlords in good buildings know it. Negotiate now, not when your lease expires.

---

## RENTS

A-grade face rents in Newcastle CBD and city fringe are tracking in the **$350–$450/sqm gross** range depending on the building, floor, and fit-out condition. B-grade space typically comes in at **$250–$330/sqm gross**.

*Note: These are estimates based on current market intelligence and comparable regional NSW data. Confirmed per-sqm figures for Newcastle NSW are not publicly reported by CBRE or Colliers at the time of writing.*

Incentives are still on the table — particularly in B-grade buildings trying to compete. Landlords in A-grade assets are pulling back on incentives as their vacancy drops. If you're in a B-grade building and your landlord is offering fit-out contributions or rent-free periods, that's a sign of their position. Use it.

Gross vs net matters here. Always ask for the gross figure. Net rents look better on paper but outgoings in older Newcastle buildings can add $80–$120/sqm on top.

---

## SUPPLY

There is **no confirmed new office supply entering the Newcastle market in 2026.** Zero.

The next meaningful supply is approximately **3,650 sqm** expected in 2027, with a further 8,000–9,000 sqm mooted beyond that — but mooted is not committed.

*Source: Property Council of Australia data, February 2026 (reported by Newcastle Herald and Cessnock Advertiser).*

This is significant for tenants. When supply is constrained and absorption stays positive, landlords hold the cards. The longer you wait, the less leverage you have.

---

## TENANT DEMAND

Net absorption in 2024 was **7,814 sqm** — one of Newcastle's strongest years on record.

The 2025 mid-year figure came in at approximately **4,836 sqm**, suggesting a more normalised pace but still solidly positive.

*Source: 4,836 sqm 2025 absorption figure — confirmed.*

The demand profile has shifted. The big enquiries are for **150–300 sqm flexible floorplates** — smaller businesses, professional services firms, and health sector operators taking quality space rather than sprawling across large floors. Corporate tenants with leases expiring are also active, often consolidating into better-located, better-fitted space.

Growth sectors driving demand in the Hunter right now:
- Health and allied health (backed by population growth and federal investment)
- Professional services (legal, accounting, consulting)
- Government and NFP
- Technology and digital firms decentralising from Sydney

The Hunter is Australia's largest regional economy. The underlying demand base is real, not speculative.

---

## WHAT THIS MEANS FOR YOU

- **If your lease expires in the next 12–18 months, start now.** The options available today will not be there when you actually need to move. Landlords know what's coming on supply. You should too.

- **Don't assume your current rent is market.** Many Newcastle businesses haven't benchmarked their lease in years. A rent review or market comparison could save you $20,000–$50,000+ over a lease term.

- **The incentive window is narrowing.** Fit-out contributions and rent-free periods are still achievable — but mainly in B-grade or secondary stock. In A-grade buildings, the deals are getting tighter. If you want leverage, act before vacancy tightens further.

Landlords in Newcastle's better buildings are aware that nothing new is coming in 2026. They are in no rush. If you walk in without a tenant rep or a competing option, you will pay more than you need to.
